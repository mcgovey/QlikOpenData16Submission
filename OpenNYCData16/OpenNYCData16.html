How does the weather affect transportation in New York City?

Public transportation is a critical tool for New York City. There are millions of subway and taxi riders each day. If we can understand how weather patterns affect public transportation, we may be able to better anticipate demand and flexibly reduce or increase capacity.

Average Daily Subway Passengers

Average Daily Taxi Passengers

As a baseline, you can see the split between subway and taxi rides average daily passengers. Without applying any filters, subway rides far outweigh taxi rides. If you select only weekends then you can see a significant drop off in subway riders and an uptick in taxi rides.

How does the temperature affect the number of subway and taxi passengers?

Across all temperature ranges, taxi rides are fairly consistent. However, when the temperature is mild (between 40 and 70 degrees fahrenheit) subway rides see an uptick.

How does precipitation affect the split of subway and taxi passengers?

This chart shows that the difference between cloud covers and sun are not significantly different. Interestingly, any precipitation causes a dip in subway rides. Looking closer at both subway and taxi rides, snow causes a dip in both methods of transportation.

On an individual day level, what is the split between taxis and subway rides?

On the x-axis are the number of daily passengers for subways plotted against daily taxi passengers on the y-axis. Each bubble represents a day. Use the buttons above to switch the color shading on the bubbles to either cloud cover or temperature.

In each New York City neighborhood, do passengers tend to take a taxi or the subway?

This map proves that the further from Manhattan, the less likely you are to use the subway. The most even split between subway and taxi passengers is on the east side of Manhattan, specifically the East Village.
